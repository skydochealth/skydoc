Aviation Telemedicine Concept

Clinical-grade support above the clouds.

SkyDoc is a physician-designed telemedicine concept for airlines — helping cabin crew and flight deck teams triage in-flight medical events, support diversion decisions, and generate clean clinical documentation.

What SkyDoc aims to solve

  • ⟡ Fragmented in-flight medical decision making
  • ⟡ Stressful “divert vs continue” choices
  • ⟡ Inconsistent documentation and follow-up

SkyDoc imagines a single, secure platform connecting cabin crew, flight deck and ground clinicians with structured protocols and clear data.

About SkyDoc

Founder-led, physician-driven aviation telemedicine concept.

SkyDoc is a concept created by Ayşe Melis Günal, a medical doctor and International Healthcare MBA candidate. The idea is born at the intersection of clinical practice, EMS emergency medicine and airline operations.

The project is currently in the exploratory and documentation phase. This website serves as a public snapshot of the idea for potential airline partners, investors and advisors.

Vision

To become the aviation sector’s trusted telemedicine layer — providing data-driven support for in-flight medical events, reducing unnecessary diversions and strengthening passenger safety.

Concept pillars

What SkyDoc would offer in a mature product.

Guided in-flight triage

Structured symptom checklists and severity scoring adapted from emergency medicine — simplified for cabin crew with limited space, equipment and time.

Diversion decision support

A decision layer that brings together clinical severity, flight time to alternates and airline policy to support the captain’s final call on diversion vs continuation.

Audit-ready documentation

Clean, time-stamped incident reports that can be securely shared with airline medical departments, insurers and regulators after landing.

Data for safety & finance

Aggregated insights over time: event types, diversion rates, cost patterns and improvement opportunities across the fleet.

Designed by a physician

Built from real clinical workflows rather than abstract software assumptions — with crew, passengers and medical directors in mind.

Expandable architecture

Future-ready concept for integration with onboard devices, ground telemedicine partners and airline IT / EHR systems.

High-level workflow

How SkyDoc could operate during an in-flight medical event.

  1. 1

    Cabin crew initiates SkyDoc

    A medical incident is reported on board. Cabin crew open the SkyDoc interface on a tablet or secure device and start a guided assessment.

  2. 2

    Structured triage & vital details

    The system prompts for key symptoms, timing, red flags and available vital signs, standardising what is communicated to ground teams.

  3. 3

    Ground clinician consultation

    A ground clinician reviews the case in real time, provides recommendations appropriate to onboard resources and flags potential need for diversion.

  4. 4

    Diversion support & documentation

    Final diversion decisions remain with the captain, but SkyDoc captures the full reasoning trail and generates a secure report for post-flight review.

SkyDoc is currently a concept. No live clinical system, product or service is being offered through this website.
